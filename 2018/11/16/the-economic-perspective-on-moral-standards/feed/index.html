For instance, much of the effort one goes through to strike is wasted if scabs cross the line (and this is why there’s a system of social censure against scabs). Breakthroughs in desirable results often have a drastically non-linear relationship to the amount of effort it takes to engage in them — as Scott has covered before with the beef vs chicken thing — and this mirrors results in domains where the goal is not ethics but something else (like chip density, as covered in another recent post).

In this sense, a low bar might keep us in stasis for much longer than is necessary — we can perpetually keep the amount of effort we’re willing to go through to make some social change below the level necessary to achieve the quorum necessary to make that change (or below the level where structural inertia eats all our progress).

This all gets mixed up when we consider that these efforts are invested into a marketplace of proxies for some global ethical-ness. Even if we assume that a global ethics exists, it’s uncontroversial that we have a hard time defining it, and that everything we do has some dubious effectiveness at some second-order proxy of ‘good behavior’. So, some part of the lower 50% will almost inevitably have some twist in their thinking that makes them in the upper 50% from their perspective — acting as brakes on the inevitable upward movement. There’s always going to be a small group of accelerationists who think giving to charity is bad because it delays the revolution (or who think being rapaciously capitalist is good because it motivates the proletariat to revolt), or some social darwinists who think that refusing to kill and eat animals will cause the breed to become weak, or paternalistic racists who think black people will be unable to manage themselves if not enslaved, or whatever.

I like the idea of carefully analyzing the bars we’re setting. But, instead of picking 50%, we probably ought to figure out what kind of effort is necessary for some concrete goal & aim for slightly higher than that amount of effort to be the norm in our clique (divided by clique size, and with the amount of effort any opposing cliques will put in added on top).
